Research Fellow · NCCU

Chia-hungTsai

I am Research Fellow of the Election Study Center at National Chengchi University. I hold Ph.D. from The Ohio State University (2003). My research focuses on electoral behavior, public opinion, democratic theory, and Taiwan's place in the Asia-Pacific security order.

Research Areas

01 ——

Electoral Behavior

Voting decisions, party identification, strategic voting, and electoral accountability in Taiwan and East Asia.

02 ——

Public Opinion

Survey methodology, cross-national opinion research, measurement theory, and public attitudes toward governance.

03 ——

Party Politics

Party systems, democratic satisfaction, ideological congruence, and political participation in new democracies.

04 ——

Security & Foreign Policy

Public alignment orientation under US-China competition, Taiwan's security dilemma, and perceptions of foreign leadership.

05 ——

American Politics

US congressional behavior, House party leadership, and American voter response to international events.

06 ——

Quantitative Methods

Bayesian inference, multilevel modeling, conjoint analysis, and panel survey design for political research.
